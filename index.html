Ishan Mishra

planetary scientist and astronomer (he/him/his)

Hi! I am a PhD candidate at Cornell University's Department of Astronomy. I am interested in statistical techniques, primarily Bayesian statistics, that help us extract the most amount of information from spectroscopic data of planetary bodies in and beyond our solar system. I am also interested in applying these techniques to asses and aid the development of future missions to advance our understanding through spectroscopy of planetary objects near and far.

I grew up in various towns in India and obtained a Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech.) degree from the Indian Institute of Technology in Guwahati, located in the foothills of the Himalayas in the north-east part of India. Although an electronics and communications engineering major in college, I was fortunate to have gotten opportunities to explore my interest in astronomy and planetary science, through various schools, workshops, and research experiences that spanned a broad range of topics, such as star clusters and main belt asteroids.

Please hop over to the Research tab to learn more about my current research in graduate school. My latest CV can be found here and my publications list on NASA/ADS can be found here.

Research

Through my time at graduate school, I have broadly focused on two research areas: Jupiter's moon Europa's surface composition and atmospheric composition of a variety of exoplanets. While the two fields I am interested in might seem disparate, they allow me to apply the same methodology, a Bayesian inference framework, to spectroscopic data of different types (surface reflection v/s atmospheric transmission) and resolution (high-res v/s low-res), helping me better understand the nuances of the physical processes as well as the Bayesian methodology. Outreach and Science Communication

I am passionate about communicating my work and astronomy to the general public and participating in efforts that help and promote marginalized communities in astronomy. over the past few years, I have mainly been involved in outreach through Astrobites and Cornell Astronomy's efforts.

Astrobites

Astrobites is an astronomy outreach website, with more than a 1000 daily visitors, that publishes daily articles summarizing latest research articles from a diverse set of subfileds within astronomy. I started as an author for the website in 2020 and you can find my articles here. I also serve as a member of the editorial committee of the Astrobites collaboration.

Outreach through Cornell Astronomy

Cornell Astronomy has a vibrant outreach culture, targetting people from all age groups, with activities spread throughout the year. I have had the great pleasure of co-organizing our flagship outreach events such as Museuem in the Dark and Expanding Your Horizons, conducting weekend exhibitions at local community centers (in the left image, you can see a couple of kids in awe of our lego transiting exoplanet demo kit at one such event), giving public talks at local libraries and observatories, and occasionaly volunteering for Ask an Astronomer, an iconic Cornell Astronomy website that answers astronomy questions from public. I am also a member of the science communication team of the Carl Sagan Institue, helping produce and edit outreach videos for its YouTube channel.
